Abasuba Community Overview

Key Facts about the Abasuba / Ebihingo ebikuru ku Abasuba
Category (English) Details (English) Ekisuba Ebisinga / Ebisinga by’obulamu (Ekisuba)
Origin The Abasuba are a Bantu ethnic group who migrated from Uganda over 500 years ago, crossing Lake Victoria to settle on islands like Rusinga and Mfangano in Kenya. Okutoka Abasuba balekireko ku Uganda okuyo obukadde 500 emyaka egisooka, nebagenda ku nnono za Rusinga ne Mfangano mu Kenya.
Location The highest concentration of Abasuba people is found on Rusinga and Mfangano islands in Lake Victoria, in Kenya's Homa Bay and Migori Counties. Ekifo Abasuba bangi balina ku nnono za Rusinga ne Mfangano mu Lake Victoria, mu Homa Bay ne Migori Counties.
Endangered language The Suba language is listed by UNESCO as endangered due to assimilation with the Luo through intermarriage and cultural influence. Lulimi olukyusibwa Lulimi lwa Suba lukyusibwa nga UNESCO lutwala nga lukyusibwa olw’okukyusa mu Luo mu mbuto n’eby’obulamu.
Revitalization efforts Community-led efforts such as Ekialo Kiona Youth Radio (Umoja Radio for Peace) help preserve Suba language and culture. Okutumbula lulimi Ebikozesebwa eby’abantu nga Ekialo Kiona Youth Radio ku Mfangano biriko okukolera ku kutereka n’okutumbula lulimi lwa Suba.
Cultural heritage The Abasuba have rich cultural heritage including storytelling, music, cuisine, and ceremonies like rainmaking. Omulimu ogw’ensiko Abasuba balina eby’obuwangwa okuli ebigambo, enyimba, mmere, n’emikolo ng’okubaga emitala egyekolerwa amaggulu.
Traditional economy They were fishermen, boat builders, and farmers, and once renowned hippo hunters. Obulimi bw’okwenyigira Mu biseera ebyakadde, Abasuba baali abakazi b’amato, abalimi, n’abavubi era baali bakwatagana n’okulwanyisa enswa.
Notable people Tom Mboya, a famous independence activist and cabinet minister, was from the Abasuba community. Abantu abamanyiddwa Omuntu amanyiddwa mu Abasuba ye Tom Mboya, omulwanirira obwenzi era nga minister omukulu mu Kenya.

Suba Language Quiz

Key Facts about the Abasuba / Ebihingo ebikuru ku Abasuba
Category (English) Details (English) Ekisuba Ebisinga / Ebisinga by’obulamu (Ekisuba)
Origin The Abasuba are a Bantu ethnic group who migrated from Uganda over 500 years ago, crossing Lake Victoria to settle on Rusinga and Mfangano islands. Okutoka Abasuba balekireko ku Uganda emyaka egisukka mu 500, nebasenguka ku Rusinga ne Mfangano mu Kenya.
Location The largest Suba population lives on Rusinga and Mfangano Islands in Lake Victoria, Kenya. Ekifo Abasuba bangi basangibwa ku Rusinga ne Mfangano mu Lake Victoria.
Cultural Heritage The Suba have a rich culture with storytelling, traditional music, food, and ceremonies like rainmaking. Obuwangwa Abasuba balina ebigambo eby’ennono, enyimba, mmere, n’emikolo ng’okubaga emitala.
Heritage Location Mfangano Island, Mawanga Rock Art Sites, Kwitutu sacred forests, Rusinga Island, and Tom Mboya Mausoleum are major Suba heritage sites. Ekifo ky’obuwangwa Mfangano, Ebifo eby’amayanja e Mawanga, ebibira ebitukuvu e Kwitutu, Rusinga, n’Emaka lya Tom Mboya bye bifo bya Suba eby’obuwangwa.
